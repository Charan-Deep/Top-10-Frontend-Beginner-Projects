Dr. Allama Iqbal

1877-1938

The Poet of the East

Dr. Allama Muhammad Iqbal, often referred to as the "Poet of the East," was a visionary philosopher, poet, and statesman whose influence transcended borders and generations. His profound ideas and poetic brilliance left an indelible mark on the intellectual and cultural landscape of not only Pakistan but the entire Muslim world. Born on November 9, 1877, in Sialkot, British India (now Pakistan), Allama Iqbal was a scholar of exceptional caliber. His early education in Sialkot, Lahore, and Europe laid the foundation for his deep understanding of both Eastern and Western philosophies. It was this synthesis of thought that set him apart as a luminary of his time.
